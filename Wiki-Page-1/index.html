Crown Shyness

Crown shyness (also canopy disengagement, canopy shyness, shyness, or inter-crown spacing spacingl3]) is a phenomenon observed in some tree species, in which the crowns of fully stocked trees do not touch each other, forming a canopy with channel-like gaps. 45 The phenomenon is most prevalent among trees of the same species, but also occurs between trees of different species. 61 [7 There exist many hypotheses as to why crown shyness is an adaptive behavior,and research suggests that it might inhibit spread of leaf-eating insect larvae. 8]
